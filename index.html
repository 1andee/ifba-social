OBJECTIVE #1

Design and implement a method to increase the number of people that submit items for you to post on IFBA sites.

the challenge

  • Few cases where clubs or individuals have contacted us to share an event or news item
  • Almost all content is posted proactively
  • Submissions are difficult to encourage (just ask Turn Out!)

how to increase submissions

  • Easiest solution is to promote the use of a distinct hashtag
  • Allow users to easily tag content with minimal effort, and for the IFBA to easily find it
  • Process could be automated with close monitoring
  • Hashtag Ideas:
    • #intfirebuff
    • #WeAreRehabAndMore
    • #WeAreTheIFBA

OBJECTIVE #2

Develop a way to teach IFBA members how to use all available social media sites.

OBJECTIVE #3

Develop a way to teach IFBA clubs how to start their own social media websites.

teaching members

  • offer cheatsheet of popular tools, platforms, apps that are frequently used by buffs
  • would require periodic review (annually) for validity
  • obvious disclaimer: recommendations are not a contact,
    no warranty implied

teaching clubs

  • offer pre-recorded tutorials with walkthroughs of basics
    • how to create an account
    • customise account with logos, headers, photos
    • how to share + post content
    • basic workflows
  • brief: ideally no more than 4 - 5 minutes each
  • similar content already exists on YouTube & Lynda.com, but we can tailor each video with a buff or canteen-related example

establish presence on instagram

  • Instagram is the dominant image sharing social network
  • Platform is owned by Facebook
  • 800+ million users; younger demographic
  • Easily integrates with other platforms:
    • Images can be automatically cross-posted onto FB/Twitter

  • Recent case study:
    • Fire Apparatus Journal created account January 2018
    • has posted/shared avg of 1 photo every 3 days
    • currently at ~1,195 followers
    • avg of 5.6 new followers/day

clubs must be flexible & willing to adapt

  • Social media platforms come and go, clubs must shift their outreach & recruiting strategy along with them
  • Teens are abandoning Facebook in dramatic numbers, study finds
  • Facebook predicted to lose 2.1 million American users under 25 this year
  • Remember MySpace?
